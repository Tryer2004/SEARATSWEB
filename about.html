About Sea Rats

Sea Rats is an underwater robotics team founded in 2024 at Miami Country Day School as part of the Underwater Robotics program. The team brings together students with a shared passion for engineering, technology, and marine science.

Through hands-on design, testing, and real-world problem solving, Sea Rats develops technical skills, teamwork, and innovation while addressing challenges related to ocean exploration and environmental conservation.

Our Mission

Our mission is to design, build, and operate underwater robots that support ocean monitoring and environmental protection while developing real-world engineering skills.

By integrating mechanical design, electronics, and programming, we aim to create reliable robotic systems capable of performing complex tasks in underwater environments. We emphasize continuous learning, iteration, and collaboration.

SeaMate Competition

In the 2025 Florida Regional SeaMate competition held in Saint Petersburg, Sea Rats earned all Scout-level awards, demonstrating strong performance across engineering, mission execution, and teamwork.

After the compeition the two original team members split up, the Sea Rats have 3 new members which means the Sea Rats will compete in Scout again this year to build our team through expirence and bond.

Our ROV

Our robot, the Le Raton, was designed with an emphasis on stability, maneuverability, and reliability in demanding underwater conditions.

The ROV utilizes closed-cell foam for buoyancy and features a balanced frame that allows for smooth and controlled movement. Its modular design enables easy maintenance and future upgrades as mission requirements evolve.

Our Claws

Our claw, now named Mouse Trap, was originally developed alongside our ROV, Le Raton. The name reflects its fast, precise gripping mechanism and compact form.

Mouse Trap is designed to efficiently grab both circular and flat objects during mission tasks. Although it was created just one week prior to the competition, it performed reliably under competition conditions.

The design was inspired by another underwater robotics enthusiast, with custom adjustments made to improve performance and durability. As the team competes at higher levels, the claw will continue to be refined to meet more advanced mission requirements.

The Mouse Trap would be used for more precise grabs in the water, so our team devoloped a new product, The Fish Trap. This claw helps us grab anything that requires trial and error in a short period of time rather then trying our Mouse Trap over and over again. This claw helps us same time and effort.

Our Camera and Its Mount

Last year, visibility was very limited while testing in the pool. This is why, this year, we are using a camera to improve visibility. To support this, we created a customized mounting system that allows for precise adjustments and stable positioning.

The camera system designed for the Scout Semate competition demonstrates a strong balance of performance, reliability, and adaptability. It has been carefully selected and optimized to deliver clear, consistent visual feedback under varying environmental conditions, ensuring accurate navigation and situational awareness during operation. Complementing the camera is a fully customized mounting solution, engineered for stability and precise alignment while minimizing vibration and allowing for easy adjustments. This tailored mount enhances both durability and functionality, making the system well-suited for the demands of the competition.

Our Custom Control Box

The customized control box has been thoughtfully designed to improve overall efficiency and usability. It provides increased internal space for better component organization, reducing clutter and simplifying maintenance or upgrades. The integration of a quick-release system further enhances practicality, allowing for rapid access and component replacement without compromising structural integrity. Together, these features create a streamlined, user-focused design that supports both performance and ease of operation.
